In addition to the statutes that require critical technology elements to be tested in a relevant environment by milestone B, there is doctrinal guidance that pertains to the Request for Proposal process and technology readiness levels.

With this guidance, program managers are able to weigh several options in terms of cost and time while ensuring that the critical technology elements meet adequate requirements.

Including the technology readiness levels in the contract language also allows for the communication of concerns regarding issues of deficiency, ambiguity, and non-compliance.

In a closed door session, Independent Review Panel members determine the technology readiness level and rationale for each element and record this information on score sheets. Copies of the score sheets are provided to the Independent Review Panel Lead and the Component Science & Technology Executive.

Each member provides his or her own technology readiness level recommendation and rationale to the group for discussion and deliberation. When providing a recommendation it’s important to take into account whether the testing was comprehensive for the sample sizes and environments measured and whether the data for the environments measured can be applied to other environments.

An opportunity is also given to the Program Management Office to provide feedback on any critical technology elements which require Technology Maturation Plans.

The Independent Review Panel Lead promotes discussions to achieve consensus on the final score and supporting rationale for a technology, but consensus is not mandated. In some cases, the Independent Review Panel Lead may have to make the decision on the final score, taking into account the unique expertise provided by each Independent Review Panel member weighed against the technology under consideration. Strong dissenting positions should be documented.

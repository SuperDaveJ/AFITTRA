Before we continue, let’s look at how the systems engineering framework plays a role in the identification of Critical Technology Elements. Systems engineering serves as the foundation of the Defense Acquisition Program, and as such, provides the context for identifying Critical Technology Elements. 

Systems engineering is defined as an interdisciplinary approach encompassing the entire technical effort to evolve and verify an integrated set of system, people, and process solutions that satisfy customer needs across the lifecycle of a system.

The key words here are evolve and verify. The process of identifying Critical Technology Elements seeks to both evolve and verify the technology element in question, making the process inextricably linked to systems engineering.

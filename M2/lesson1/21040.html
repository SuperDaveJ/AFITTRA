TRA Process > Initiate a TRA
4/7

Output Activities

  1. The PM and Component S&T Executive work together to establish the schedule and budget for the TRA.
  2. The Component S&T Executive identifies the program action officers.
  3. The PM provides recommendations for potential Independent Review Panel (IRP) candidates.
  4. Deputy Assistant Secretary for Engineering and Technical Management (SAF / AQRE) compiles subject matter expert (SME) information for IRP candidates.

Schedule

The TRA schedule:

  • Varies with the program’s acquisition strategy and takes into account any source selection or down-select activity
    • Activity start points and duration may vary greatly
  • Varies as a function of Component procedures
    • Larger programs typically take a full year or more. Smaller, less complex programs normally require less time.

Budget

The TRA budget must consider:

  • IRP member support
  • Travel
  • Contractor support
  • Technology demonstrations
  • Assessment functional costs
  • Conference fees
  • Critical Technology Element (CTE) briefings
  • Other costs that vary from program to program

Action Officers

The Component S&T Executive identifies the following program action officers:

  • Deputy Assistant Secretary for Engineering and Technical Management (SAF / AQRE) – TRA process co-owner
  • Deputy Assistant Secretary for Science and Technology (SAF / AQRT) – Air Force Research Laboratory (AFRL) interface

Independent Review Panel (IRP) candidates

Based upon the technological subject matter to be assessed, the PM provides recommendations for potential Independent Review Panel (IRP) candidates to the SAF / AQRE.

Subject matter expert (SME) information

The SAF / AQRE reviews the PM’s IRP recommendations, and then compiles the SME biographical information.

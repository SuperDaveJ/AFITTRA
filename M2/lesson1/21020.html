TRA Initiation Directives

Title 10 United States Code (USC) 2366b sets the ground work for the TRA program with the statement, "the technology in the program has been demonstrated in a relevant environment".

DoDI 5000.02 contains the requirements and procedures for Acquisition Category (ACAT) programs. For programs with critical technology elements that are ACAT I or have the potential to become ACAT I, the initiation of a TRA is statutory as mandated by Congress.

ACAT II and III acquisition programs have a regulatory mandate that requires the initiation of a TRA.

Title 10 United States Code (USC) 2366b

Sec. 2366b. Major defense acquisition programs: certification required before Milestone B or Key Decision Point B approval

(a) Certification.— A major defense acquisition program may not receive Milestone B approval, or Key Decision Point B approval in the case of a space program, until the milestone decision authority—

(2) further certifies that—

(D) "the technology in the program has been demonstrated in a relevant environment"

DoDI 5000.02

ACAT I

A major Defense Acquisition Program that is estimated to require an eventual total expenditure of:

  • More than $365 million in fiscal year (FY) 2000 constant dollars for research, development, test, and evaluation
  • More than $2.190 billion in FY constant dollars for procurement

ACAT II

A major system that does not meet the criteria for ACAT I, but is estimated to require an eventual expenditure of:

  • More than $140 million in FY 2000 constant dollars for research, development, test, and evaluation
  • More than $660 million in FY constant dollars for procurement

ACAT III

This is any system that does not meet the criteria for ACAT II or above.

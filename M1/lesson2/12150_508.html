What is a role or responsibility of each of the individuals in a TRA process?

DRD

Provides DDR&E recommendations concerning certification

SAF / AQR

Performs a peer review on the TRA Report

SAF / AQRE

Reviews MDAP TRAs one month prior to scheduled MD date

PM at PMO

Collects data for the IRP to use in assessing CTE maturity

IRP Team Members

Develops a list of final CTEs

Contractor Liaison

Attains technical data from contractors

Additional IRP Contributors

Participate within the IRP or act as SMEs

Which policies and directives affect a TRA?

TRA policies and directives include:

  • AFI 63-101
  • AFI 99-103
  • TRA Deskbook
  • Defense Acquisition Guidebook
  • DoDD 5000.01
  • DoDI 5000.02
  • Title 10 United States Code (USC) 2366

What are the TRA activities that are appropriate for each phase of the acquisition lifecycle?

The month and activity information is as follows:

  • 12 to 24 months prior to MD: PM requests TRA
  • 12 months prior to MD: AQR and PM develop plan
  • 11 months prior to MD: AQR coordinates IRP team
  • 10 months prior to MD: PM identifies candidate CTEs
  • 9 months prior to MD: AQR and IRP review CTE list
  • 8 months prior to MD: AQR coordinates plan with DRD (ACAT ID and IAM only)
  • 8 to 6 months prior to MD: PM collects data for CTE assessment
  • 6 months prior to MD: IRP assigns TRLs
  • No later than 3 months prior to MD: PM and IRP Lead document TRA finding and coordinate with AQR; and for ACAT ID and IAM, with DRD.
  • No later than 2.5 months prior to MD: PM submits TRA to AQR
  • No later than 2 months prior to MD: AQR submits TRA to AFAE; and for ACAT ID and IAM, with DRD.
  • Milestone Decision

What are ways that the TRA can be integrated into contract language?

The TRA contract language requirements include:

  • The PM shall include language in solicitations for the EMD phase advising offerors that no contract will be awarded to them if they have not demonstrated in a relevant environment and evidence of TRL and relevant environment demonstration must be provided in their proposals
  • Expectations for warfighters, users, decision-makers, evaluation teams, and industry from the outset of the source selection and a clear understanding of the methods of estimating costs for the program including uncertainty analysis and verification requirements
  • Establishment of the minimum performance or capability requirements against which offers will be judged and identification of the key discriminators from among the mission requirements

What are the ways the perceptual lens affects the TRA process?

  • Different people have different viewpoints; and therefore work in different corporate cultures.
  • Different things get measured in the different corporate cultures.
  • The IRP Lead needs to deliver a fair and accurate TRA by focusing the positive aspects of the perceptual lens into a COP.

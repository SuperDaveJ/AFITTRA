TRA Process Stakeholders

TRA stakeholders include:

  • Director, Research Directorate (DRD)
  • Deputy Assistant Secretary of the Air Force for Science, Technology and Engineering (SAF / AQR)
  • Deputy Assistant Secretary of the Air Force for Engineering (SAF / AQRE)
  • Program Manager (PM) at the Program Management Office (PMO)
  • Independent Review Panel (IRP) Lead
  • IRP Team Members
  • Contractor Liaison
  • Additional IRP Contributors

Director, Research Directorate (DRD)

The DRD is subordinate to Director, Defense Research and Engineering (DDR&E). The DDR&E will sometimes delegate responsibility for briefing the Under Secretary of Defense for Acquisition, Technology, and Logistics (USD (AT&L)) to the DRD.

Assistant Secretary of the Air Force for Science, Technology and Engineering (SAF / AQR)

The SAF / AQR is also known as the Component Service (Air Force (AF) Science and Technology (S&T) Executive.

Deputy Assistant Secretary of the Air Force for Engineering (SAF / AQRE)

The SAF / AQRE is the Action Officer for Acquisition and the Product Center’s point of contact (POC).

Program Manager (PM) at Program Management Office (PMO)

An individual designated by DODD 5000.01 who has the authority to accomplish program objectives for development, productions and sustainment to meet user’s operational needs. The PM has total lifecycle system management authority.

IRP Lead

IRP Lead will be an experienced technical leader such as an engineer or a scientist and will be a subject matter expert (SME) from outside of the Program Executive Officer (PEO) chain of command.

IRP Team Members

Team members are SMEs that have knowledge of program’s technologies and are independent of program’s technology development.

Potential sources of IRP Team Members are Center Engineers, Air Force Research Laboratory (AFRL), Federally Funded Research and Development Centers (FFRDCs), Advisory and Assistance Services (A&AS), and other Services.

Contractor Liaison

The Contractor Liaison coordinates with contractors for TRA data, when necessary.

Additional IRP Contributors

A cockpit review team, battlefield airmen, warfighters from other Services, maintenance personnel, or logistics personnel may be contributors to the TRA.

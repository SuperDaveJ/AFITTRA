Why is the TRA Process Needed?

Text popups for each of the four selectable contributing factors on the left side of the page display on the right side of the page in a framed text area.

Aging Workforce

Because many members of the acquisition workforce are at or near retirement age, the AF is in danger of suffering a loss of knowledge very quickly. There are very few qualified replacements prepared to take the place of the retiring workforce.

Budget Cuts

Many of the policies and programs intended to enhance our infrastructure development have experienced significant budget cuts.

Lack of Experience Managing Technological Development / Risk

The AF has lost the recipe on how to manage technological development and its risk. This may be due to a limited understanding of:

  • The most accurate models
  • Domains (e.g., physical, informational, cognitive, social, knowledgeable, etc.) and their applicability to models and technology development
  • Ontologies which describe domains and taxonomies which classify items within the ontologies and how they apply to models and technology development. For instance, a database is a collection of information on a subject area (ontology). That database is designed according to how one looks for a particular item within the database (taxonomy). This is how equipment such as radars and electronic warfare systems identify their radio frequency detections.

Diminishing Technology Infrastructure

Successful technology development is a time-consuming process. It may take up to 20 years to develop and implement a new technological advancement.

Without a robust Air Force Research and Development Program today, there will be no way to catch up 20 years from now.

Today's technology plans must try to anticipate what will be needed to face threats not yet imagined. For example, the current or future warfighter will be dealing with Military Operations Other Than War (MOOTW) or nonconventional warfare. Our conventional warfare and technology might only work in limited circumstances. The AF might not be able to get its infrastructure back, but it still needs to manage and mitigate technological development risk.
